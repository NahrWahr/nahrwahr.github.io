We'll have $>50$% of global energy needs met by solar by 2035.

We need solar to double every $\approx 2.5$ years to get to the target.

It currently doubles every $\approx 2.7$ years.

Assuming a very modest speed up, current extrapolations is we are on track to
get there by 2040, but bet is push for energy breaks the slow down in growth in
2030s.

The required multiplier for total solar output is:
$$
\text{Growth Factor} = \frac{N(t)}{N_0} = \frac{35,000}{2,100} \approx 16.6
$$

Using the standard exponential growth formula:
$$
N(t) = N_0 \cdot 2^{\frac{t}{T_d}}
$$
